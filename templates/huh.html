Emails identify you.

The Problem

We often use the same email address when we register on a website or sign up for a service, making it an unique piece of information about us. Two websites that want to trade information about their users, use email addresses as personal identifiers. It's the one piece of information that every website asks for.

Web analytics companies and advertisers are increasingly turning to social media to mine for data about you. They can find your public profiles and work out your age, where you live and what you had for lunch yesterday. They can build a profile about every user of their customer's website. On your second visit to a website they know as much about you as your friends - but you might not have realised that.

What Inboxen Is?

Inboxen is a service that provides you with an infinate number [1] of unique inboxes. It's not designed your replace your personal email address, but rather compliment it. Every time you sign up on a new website, click the "Add Inbox" button to create another email address. This leaves your personal address free from promotional offers and other junk mail.
